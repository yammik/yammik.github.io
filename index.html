hello welcome

Before becoming a developer, I was in graduate school for microbiology. Here, aside from insane character building and grit, I learned critical thinking, research design, and scientific writing.

I studied biology in undergrad. Wanted to be a veterinarian at the time, so did not decide on a concentration. which worked out for the best, because I got the best of both worlds.

My favorite memories of studying ecology are: (1) running around the campus with a bug net to make my collection for entomology, catching things like the monarch butterfly and (2) waking up at 4am to go birdwatching all around upstate NY, where I got to see the great blue heron and the red-tailed hawk, to name a few.

Molecular biology was more indoors benchwork, which was still fun. I eventually got to manipulate DNA of bacteria in grad school, and made glowing bacteria with green-fluorescent protein (GFP).

My experience in life sciences was great, but I needed more. I needed a career where I could create things. Beautiful, meaningful things. A friend recommended that I try coding. Thus began my affair with coding.

Python was my first language (the firsts are always special) (thank you codecademy). Turns out, scientific writing and coding have a lot of similarities (more on that in a future blog post). Constructing ideas and figuring out how to implement them into code quickly became my favorite part of the day. Also, one of the greatest frustrations in the lab was not being able to figure out why experiments went wrong. Debugging was like a dream come true in that sense.
