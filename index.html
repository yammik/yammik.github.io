hello, welcome

I am a full stack web developer primarily on Ruby on Rails and ReactJS.
Advocate of poutine, ramen, tacos, and earl grey ice cream.
Also called the Mother of Dead Jokes. Like, not morbid jokes. Just...jokes that kill the conversation.

Before becoming a developer, I was in graduate school for microbiology. Here, aside from building character and grit, I learned critical thinking, research design, and scientific writing (which is a lot like programmatic thinking).

I studied biology in undergrad. I wanted to be a veterinarian at the time, so I did not decide on a concentration (either ecology or cell&molecular biology), which worked out for the best, because I got the best of both worlds.

My favorite memories of studying ecology are: (1) running around the campus with a bug net to make my collection for entomology, catching things like the monarch butterfly and (2) waking up at 4am to go birdwatching all around upstate NY, where I got to see the great blue heron and the red-tailed hawk, to name a few.

Molecular biology was more indoors benchwork, which was still fun. I eventually got to manipulate DNA of bacteria in grad school, and made glowing bacteria with green-fluorescent protein (GFP).

My experience in life sciences was great, but I needed more. I needed a career where I could create things. Beautiful, meaningful things. A friend recommended that I try coding. Thus began my affair with coding.

Python was my first language (the firsts are always special) (thank you codecademy).

Turns out, scientific writing and coding have a lot of similarities (more on that in a future blog post). Constructing ideas and figuring out how to implement them into code quickly became my favorite part of the day.

Also, one of the greatest frustrations in the lab was not being allowed to figure out why experiments went wrong, because you would need to access some parallel universe to tweak exactly one variable to determine its role in the process.
Debugging was like a dream come true in that sense. I can intentionally break a line in the program and have it return to a working version at no cost?
The program actually tells me where things went wrong, down to the line? /* mindblown */

Currently, I am looking for a position in a team where I can contribute and grow! My main interest is in education and healthcare.

Sometimes I say 'what a time to be alive' jokingly, but in all seriousness. What a time to be alive. The way technology is shaping the world is amazing.
Education softwares are changing the way children are taught, in and out of the classroom. And they're the future of our country and the world.

Likewise, softwares being used in the healthcare industry can literally save lives in the downstream process. Applications are not just programs for a select few to be familiar with anymore. They have become an integral way of our lives and interactions with the world.

And we're putting that power into the hands of developers. We can do this.
I am excited to provide the skills I have now to contribute to this mission.

sandboCSS | (pronounced 'sandbox'), a CSS cheatsheet that makes CSS code for you

demo here
Built with: ReactJS, resize-and-drag, styled-components, copy-and-paste

classic mode displays most CSS properties you can set on an element in one panel, so you don't have to know what properties you can use beforehand.
Once you click on a property, all of the available options appear so you don't have to look it up.
Clicking applies the designated style to the currently selected element, so you can see for yourself what a certain CSS property does (or doesn't do).

interactive mode is in beta stage. The resize- and reposition-drag are wonky as of the time of this writing.
The biggest challenge has been applying CSS style that the use might want on the draggable and resizable element, which already gets some styles from the RnD package. It's a WIP but I will make it happen
